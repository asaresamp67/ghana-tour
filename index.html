Brief History of Ghana

Empire of Ancient Ghana

The Empire of Ghana was located in Western Africa in what is today the countries of Mauritania, Senegal, and Mali. The region lies just south of the Sahara Desert and is mostly savanna grasslands. Major rivers in the region such as the Gambia River, Senegal River, and the Niger River served as the means of transportation and trade.

Ghana in the modern era

Ghana is a West African country on the Gulf of Guinea. The country is known for its species-rich wildlife, the old forts and lonely beaches, for example in Busua. In the coastal towns of Elmina and Cape Coast, there are posubans (local shrines), colonial buildings and former castles that have been converted into museums and are reminiscent of the slave trade. North of Cape Coast is the extensive Kakum National Park.
